Project overview

Using data can lead to more impactful decision making over intuition alone. But collecting, analyzing, and visualizing data is difficult, especially in resource constrained settings. The Disease Surveillance and Risk Monitoring (DiSARM) project is building an open-source platform that enables disease control programs to better target interventions spatially, guide field teams more precisely, and evaluate coverage more robustly. The DiSARM platform is comprised of a web-based application and a set of customized algorithms accessible through an application programming interface (API).

The DiSARM project is lead by the Malaria Elimination Initiative at the University of California San Francisco in collaboration with Peoplesized and the Clinton Health Access Initiative. The project is supported by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, Task Force on Global Health, and Google.
